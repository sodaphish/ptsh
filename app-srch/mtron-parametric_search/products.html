Crystals

A crystal is an electronic component based on quartz technology. The properties of quartz allow for precise timing over a broad temperature range. The frequency of a crystal can remain as precise as +/- 20ppm or 0.002% over a temperature range of -40 to 85 C. A crystal must be placed in an oscillator circuit in order to operate.

Frequency: The main component and purpose of a crystal. Cycles per second generated or Hz. This parameter is usually specified in MegaHertz(MHz)

Tolerance: The accuracy of the crystal at 25 C, a parameter needed to allow for error in quartz manufacturing process. Specified in parts per million(ppm)

Stability: The accuracy achieved over the operating temperature range so, naturally, a temperature range must also be specified. Specified in parts per million(ppm)

Tolerance and stability are specified as maximum values and added together to represent the absolute maximum frequency drift of the crystal.

Temperature range: Operating temperature range of the crystal. Specified in degrees Celsius (C).

Shunt capacitance: The internal capacitive value of the crystal. Specified in pico Farads (pF).

ESR: Effective Series Resistance or internal resistive value of the quartz. Specified in Ohms.

Load capacitance: The value of the external load needed in order for the crystal to run at optimal operation. Specified in pico Farads (pF)

Drive Level: Maximum power rating of crystal. Specified in max milliWatts(mW). Exceeding this rating could damage the crystal.

Package: M-tron has a variety of crystal packages available in surface mount and through hole configurations.



Oscillators

Crystal oscillators are electronic components based on quartz crystals. In order to get a crystal to operate, an oscillator circuit must be utilized. These packaged oscillators eliminate the need to design an oscillator circuit in order to get a crystal to function. Because they are based on quartz crystals, the same frequency vs. temperature characteristics are observed.

Temperature Range: Expected operating temperature of the device. Specified in degrees Celsius (C).

Stability: Absolute frequency drift expected to occur. In most cases this includes tolerance at 25 (C), frequency vs. temperature, load, supply voltage. Specified in parts per million (ppm).

Output type: Fixed or Tristate or Dual Complementary. Fixed output is always outputting a signal as long as power is applied. Tristate refers to being able to "disable" the output into a state of high impedance. Dual complementary option is popular in ECL/PECL logic and has outputs Q and Q-not.

Symmetry/Logic: Logic Compatibility of output and duty cycle or symmetry of waveform.

Package: M-tron has a variety of oscillator packages available in surface mount and through hole configurations.

Aging: The ability to stay within tolerance over a long period of time.



VCXOs

Voltage Controlled Crystal Oscillators (VCXOs) are oscillators with the ability to vary in frequency by varying the level of a control voltage. These VCXOs can be varied by as much as +/- 300ppm or 0.03% of its center frequency.

Temperature Range: Expected operating temperature of the device. Specified in degrees Celsius (C).

Stability: Absolute frequency drift expected to occur. In most cases this includes tolerance at 25 (C), frequency vs. temperature, load, supply voltage. Specified in parts per million (ppm).

Output type: Tristate or Voltage controlled or Dual Complementary. Voltage controlled is a non-tristate output that is only controlled by a control voltage. Tristate refers to being able to "disable" the output into a state of high impedance while still having the voltage control option. Dual complementary option is popular in ECL/PECL logic and has outputs Q and Q-not.

Pull Range: The total amount of variance the VCXO can be offset from its center frequency. Specified in parts per million (ppm).

Symmetry/Logic: Logic Compatibility of output and duty cycle or symmetry of waveform.

Package: M-tron has a variety of oscillator packages available in surface mount and through hole configurations.

Aging: The ability to stay within tolerance over a long period of time.



TCXOs and DCXOs

Temperature Compensated Crystal Oscillators (TCXOs) are oscillators with the ability to achieve up to +/- 1ppm stability. A DCXO, or Digitally Compensated Crystal Oscillator, is TCXO using digital techniques to provide stability of +/- 0.1ppm.

Temperature Range: Expected operating temperature of the device. Specified in degrees Celsius (C).

Stability: Absolute frequency drift expected to occur. In most cases this includes tolerance at 25 (C), frequency vs. temperature, load, supply voltage. Specified in parts per million (ppm).

Output type: Fixed or Voltage controlled. Fixed output is always outputting a signal as long as power is applied. Voltage controlled option allows for additional tuning of a TCXO with the use of a control voltage pin.

Symmetry/Logic: Logic Compatibility of output and duty cycle or symmetry of waveform. In TCXOs, in addition to logic output, a sinewave or clipped sinewave output is also available.

Package: M-tron has a variety of oscillator packages available in surface mount and through hole configurations.

Aging: The ability to stay within tolerance over a long period of time.
